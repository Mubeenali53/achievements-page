Projects

Welcome to the innovative hub of TSIG MJCET, where revolutionary projects push the limits of technology. Our club is committed to nurturing creativity, fostering collaboration, and promoting technical excellence in four forefront domains: VLSI, DSAI, FSD, and Cybersecurity. Within this hub, we strive to redefine boundaries, encouraging members to engage in groundbreaking initiatives that contribute to the ever-evolving landscape of technology.

IIOT Conveyor Belt

The IIoT conveyor belt project utilizes Jetson Nano, a webcam, and Python for image recognition in industrial processes. This advanced system identifies and segregates products on the conveyor belt in real-time, employing AI for decision-making. The robotic arm, controlled by the system, picks and places products with precision, showcasing the potential of edge computing and machine learning in smart manufacturing. The integration of Jetson Nano enhances computational power, enabling efficient and accurate product handling, thereby optimizing industrial automation processes for increased productivity and reduced manual errors.

Scara

Welcome to the forefront of automation innovation with the TSIG SCARA project, a groundbreaking fusion of cutting-edge hardware and intelligent software. Engineered for precision, the SCARA robot features state-of-the-art actuators, sensors, and a robust mechanical structure to deliver unparalleled accuracy in assembly tasks. Its high-speed capabilities are powered by advanced servo motors, ensuring rapid and precise movements. On the software front, our intuitive programming interface simplifies task configuration, reducing the learning curve for operators.

The SCARA project's software is designed for seamless integration, featuring adaptive algorithms for real-time adjustments and collaborative capabilities for efficient human-robot interaction. With a commitment to both hardware excellence and intelligent software solutions, TSIG's SCARA project is poised to redefine the landscape of automation, bringing forth a new era of precision, versatility, and efficiency.

Digital Twin

Digital Twin the project showcases a dual robotic arm system designed for precise object manipulation. The synchronized arms work in coordination, offering versatility for complex tasks. With an emphasis on mobile control, users can operate and monitor the arms remotely using a mobile device. Incorporating digital twin technology allows for virtual simulation and optimization before practical implementation. This prototype holds promise for automation in various industries, providing scalability and a user-friendly design for improved operational efficiency.

Food Serving Bot

The FoodServing Bot project leverages Arduino technology and Bluetooth communication to create an automated food serving system. The Arduino microcontroller, at the project's core, manages the serving mechanism with precision servo motors. Users can wirelessly control the bot using Bluetooth-enabled devices, ensuring flexibility and convenience. The modular design allows customization, while a user-friendly interface enhances interaction. Safety features and power efficiency are integrated, and future enhancements could expand its capabilities, making it a versatile solution for various food service applications.

Telepresence

The Telepresence Bot project is an innovative mobile-operated robot designed for remote control via WiFi connectivity. Users can operate the robot using their smartphones or other WiFi-enabled devices, gaining real-time access to its surroundings through a mounted camera. This project is ideal for applications such as surveillance, exploration, or remote monitoring where physical presence may be challenging. The seamless integration of mobile control, stable WiFi connectivity, and live camera feed make the Telepresence Bot a versatile and user-friendly solution for various scenarios requiring remote presence.

Humanoid

This innovative bot seamlessly integrates with users' smartphones or devices, allowing them to transmit exercise commands effortlessly. The Bluetooth connectivity ensures a reliable and responsive communication channel between the user and the bot. Users can simply send walking exercise instructions to the bot, and it adeptly interprets and executes them, providing a dynamic and personalized exercise experience. This humanoid bot not only showcases the potential of human-robot interaction but also promotes a convenient and engaging way for individuals to incorporate physical activity into their daily routines. With its user-friendly interface and efficient Bluetooth connectivity, this bot exemplifies the intersection of technology and fitness, making exercise accessible and enjoyable for users of all levels.

Competitions & Winning

Aakruti

Aakruti is an annual design competition organized by TSIG MJCET, where students unleash their creativity in graphic, user interface, and product design. Emphasizing user-centric and sustainable practices, Aakruti goes beyond celebrating design excellence. Participants benefit from workshops and guest lectures by industry professionals, gaining exposure to the latest trends and tools. Aakruti serves as a launchpad for aspiring designers, providing a platform to showcase innovative perspectives and aesthetic flair in the creative world.

E-yantra

e-Yantra is a robotics competition challenging TSIG MJCET members to design and build innovative robotic solutions for real-world problems. It involves a multidisciplinary approach, incorporating electronics, programming, and mechanical design. Participants gain hands-on experience, develop problem-solving skills, and showcase teamwork. The involvement of TSIG MJCET reflects a commitment to fostering innovation and practical engineering application.

Hack-Reveloution

HackRevolution, a prominent hackathon, sees active participation from TSIG MJCET. The event features an intense coding marathon where teams collaborate to address real-world challenges across four domains: hardware, software, healthcare, and fintech. Beyond coding skills, participants are encouraged to apply critical thinking and present effective solutions. Challenges incorporate emerging technologies like AI, blockchain, and cybersecurity. In addition to enhancing coding experience, participation offers networking opportunities with industry professionals in specific domains, paving the way for potential internships and collaborations.

ISRO Robotics Competition

IRoC-U 2024, organized by ISRO's U R Rao Satellite Centre, encourages innovative robotic rover designs from Indian youth in academia and industry. Following successful lunar missions, the challenge focuses on space robotics, AI, and ML with the theme "Let's build a space robot." It involves engineering projects, providing a standardized platform, and fostering collaboration for future technologies. Students from TSIG MJCET can enhance skills in problem-solving, engineering design, and collaboration. Teams must build rovers meeting specific specifications, performing tasks like sample picking and target identification. IRoC-U 2024 offers TSIG MJCET students an opportunity to contribute to space robotics advancements through collaboration with ISRO.
