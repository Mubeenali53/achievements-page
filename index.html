Competitions & Winning

Aakruti

Aakruti is an annual design competition organized by TSIG MJCET, where students unleash their creativity in graphic, user interface, and product design. Emphasizing user-centric and sustainable practices, Aakruti goes beyond celebrating design excellence. Participants benefit from workshops and guest lectures by industry professionals, gaining exposure to the latest trends and tools. Aakruti serves as a launchpad for aspiring designers, providing a platform to showcase innovative perspectives and aesthetic flair in the creative world.

E-yantra

e-Yantra is a robotics competition challenging TSIG MJCET members to design and build innovative robotic solutions for real-world problems. It involves a multidisciplinary approach, incorporating electronics, programming, and mechanical design. Participants gain hands-on experience, develop problem-solving skills, and showcase teamwork. The involvement of TSIG MJCET reflects a commitment to fostering innovation and practical engineering application.

Hack-Reveloution

HackRevolution, a prominent hackathon, sees active participation from TSIG MJCET. The event features an intense coding marathon where teams collaborate to address real-world challenges across four domains: hardware, software, healthcare, and fintech. Beyond coding skills, participants are encouraged to apply critical thinking and present effective solutions. Challenges incorporate emerging technologies like AI, blockchain, and cybersecurity. In addition to enhancing coding experience, participation offers networking opportunities with industry professionals in specific domains, paving the way for potential internships and collaborations.

ISRO Robotics Competition

IRoC-U 2024, organized by ISRO's U R Rao Satellite Centre, encourages innovative robotic rover designs from Indian youth in academia and industry. Following successful lunar missions, the challenge focuses on space robotics, AI, and ML with the theme "Let's build a space robot." It involves engineering projects, providing a standardized platform, and fostering collaboration for future technologies. Students from TSIG MJCET can enhance skills in problem-solving, engineering design, and collaboration. Teams must build rovers meeting specific specifications, performing tasks like sample picking and target identification. IRoC-U 2024 offers TSIG MJCET students an opportunity to contribute to space robotics advancements through collaboration with ISRO.
